Not all math questions on the SAT are multiple-choice. For one type  called a student-produced response (or grid-in) -- you have to find and fill in your own answer. No Penalty for Wrong
Answers No points will be subtracted for a wrong answer to this question-type, since there are no multiple-choice options. Compared to Other Question-Types Grid-ins are like regular math
questions. They cover the same subject areas, and they range in difficulty from easy to hard. Have a Calculator on Hand If you use a calculator to solve the answer to a problem, check your
work carefully. How Do You Prepare?
