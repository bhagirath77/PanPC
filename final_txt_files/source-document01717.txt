Bachelor's Master's Associate's programs are designed for students who seek a two-year degree as either their final degree in higher education or the foundation for futher study at the
bachelor's level. Students must possess a high school diploma or its equivalent to be admitted to the Associate's program. AMU offers Bachelor of Science and Bachelor of Arts degrees that
require between 120 and 124 semester hours depending on the program. Students must have a high school diploma or its equivalent to be admitted to a bachelor's degree program. Master's
programs range from 33 to 48 semester hoursof study and require either a comprehensive exam, a thesis, or an integrative practicum. Students must have completed a bachelors degree from
an accredited institution to be admitted to a master's program.
